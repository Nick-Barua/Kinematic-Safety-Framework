## 🎯 Overview
The **Kinematic Safety Framework (KSF)** is a unified mathematical and algorithmic architecture designed to extract actionable truth from high-entropy, noisy environments. By transposing **Phase-Space Analysis**—originally developed for reconstructing the evolutionary history of 468,000 stars—into real-time autonomous systems, KSF provides a proactive engine for terrestrial and aerial safety.

## 🔄 The "Space-to-Street" Logic
My research demonstrates that the deconvolution of integrated galactic spectra shares a fundamental mathematical DNA with the deconvolution of human pose sequences. Whether mapping a galaxy or a street corner, the goal is **Kinematic Reconstruction.**

## 📐 Core Mathematical Pillars

### 1. Dynamic Risk Quantification (RASA)
Central to the framework is the real-time risk assessment equation used to bridge the gap between SATCOM latency and autonomous decision-making:

$$R(t) = \alpha \cdot U_{sensor}(t) + \beta \cdot L_c(t)$$

* $U_{sensor}(t)$: Multi-modal uncertainty (LWIR Thermal + NIR Stereo).
* $L_c(t)$: Communication/System latency coefficients.

### 2. ISO 26262 & Functional Safety (FuSa)
KSF maps clinical **Hazard Analysis (HARA)** derived from 1,258 medical incidents (Sudden Incapacitation) to **ASIL-D** safety goals. This transitions ADAS from *reactive* braking to *proactive* prevention by identifying pre-fall staggering indicators 0.3–0.8s before ground contact.
